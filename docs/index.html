NCEA Level 2 - AS 91263: Design a questionnaire

Unit Overview

This unit guides students through the process of designing a robust and effective questionnaire for a statistical investigation. The primary focus is on the principles of design, ensuring the final questionnaire is fit for its intended purpose. This interactive guide breaks down the unit plan into its core components to support teaching and learning.

Achievement Standard

AS 91263

Credits

3

Duration

~ 3 Weeks

Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this unit, students will be able to:

  • Understand the purpose and role of questionnaires within the PPDAC cycle.
  • Identify and differentiate between various types of questionnaire questions.
  • Formulate clear, concise, unbiased, and relevant questions.
  • Recognise and avoid common pitfalls in question wording.
  • Structure a questionnaire logically, including an introduction, main body, and conclusion.
  • Consider and address ethical implications in questionnaire design.
  • Design a questionnaire that is "fit for purpose" for a given statistical investigation.

The PPDAC Cycle

Questionnaire design is a crucial part of the 'Data' stage within the broader Statistical Investigation Process, known as the PPDAC cycle. This cycle helps structure any statistical inquiry.

→
Plan
→
Data

→
Conclusion

(The cycle typically flows back from Conclusion to Problem for new investigations)

Assessment Guide

This section details the requirements for the summative assessment. Use the tabs below to understand the specific criteria for achieving at each grade level. The final submission must be a complete questionnaire that is well-designed and fit for its stated purpose.

Achievement Criteria

Design a questionnaire for a statistical investigation.

  • Includes a clear purpose.
  • Uses appropriate question types.
  • Has a logical flow.

Design a well-designed questionnaire for a statistical investigation.

  • All criteria for Achieved are met.
  • Questions are clear and unbiased.
  • Uses appropriate scales.
  • Shows consideration for the target audience.

Design a well-designed questionnaire that is fit for purpose.

  • All criteria for Merit are met.
  • Effectively achieves its stated purpose.
  • Proactively addresses potential issues (e.g., non-response, sensitive questions).
  • Demonstrates a sophisticated understanding of design principles.

Summative Assessment Requirements

The submitted questionnaire must include:

  • A clear statement of the purpose of the statistical investigation.
  • Identification of the target population.
  • A concise and informative introduction for respondents.
  • A variety of appropriate question types relevant to the investigation.
  • Clear, unbiased, and unambiguous wording for all questions.
  • A logical flow and structure that guides the respondent.
  • Evidence of consideration of ethical issues (e.g., a statement about anonymity/confidentiality).
  • A brief conclusion/thank you message.
